DEGEN

Lindsay Degen's passion for knitting began at the age of three when her grandmother gave her a set of knitting needles and taught her how to stitch. Over the years Degen honed her skills and made a name for herself in her hometown of Mariemont, Ohio, weaving basic thread into unique pieces, each telling a story about her life and experience. In September 2011, Lindsay launched a capsule collection of knit bras, underwear and socks. In 2012, she added sweaters, dresses, leggings and even shoes. The SS13 collection, titled The Revolution Will Be Craftivised, Lindsay embraces the individuality of street style with the Do It Yourself movement.

Flutter by Jill Golden

Flutter by Jill Golden is a collection of costume jewelry handmade in New York City with a directional point-of-view. Each season, Golden takes inspiration from her far-flung travels, translating the look and feel of places such as Paris, Morocco and Barcelona into her signature bold yet feminine jewelry.

For SS13, Golden takes inspiration from the Aegean Coast of Turkey. In addition, Flutter will launch its second Isaro by Flutter collection in partnership with Indego Africa and Rwandan Artisans.

J Koo

J Koo is designer brand comprising Jinwoo Choi and Yeonjoo Koo, launched in the UK in February 2010. Jason Scott

Since its 2012 launch, Jason Scott has maintained a vision of creating everyday clothing that combines a casual sensibility with modern, tailored design. Inspired by a desire to redefine contemporary menswear, the collection is wearable pieces that reflect a stylish, downtown attitude complete with expert tailoring, luxurious fabrics and attention to detail.

Jason Scott is designed and produced entirely in Los Angeles. Scott offers a full range of men's ready-to-wear with plans for a women's collection in the near future as well as a Jason Scott flagship store in the US.

Booth 3254

Jyune

 Jyune is contemporary women's wear collection, established in New York. It is urban chic casual wear, heavily focused on delicate knitwear and cashmere. Jyune is for the woman a la mode, who desires to express her personality and individuality in everyday life. It is edgy, whimsical and fashionable, yet timeless and easy to care for. They are perfect for city girls with dynamic, hip and trendy lifestyles.

Kill City

 For Spring 2013, Kill City Jeans takes us through decades of motorcycle culture. Split into three deliveries, each round is inspired by a different type of moto theme The first delivery features Kenneth Anger inspired classic styles like vests, leather, sheerness, studs, and the classic motorcycle jacket. The second delivery is inspired by the 1970s and has an Easy Rider vibe to it with a patched denim vest and military jacket. The brand invites you to further customize the pieces with your own patches. The third delivery features the more athletic, clean lines of urban 90s motorcycle culture. Lots of the pieces were reminiscent of Motocross and others were inspired by muses such as Yolandi of Die Antwoord. Ride on Kill City Spring 2013!

Not by Jenny Lai

 Not is an experimental womenswear brand focused on heightening the visceral experience of dressing. Believing that clothing is a subtlety of theatre in our lives, Not provides the modern woman with a tactile exploration of performance and function. Before forming her own studio, Jenny Lai strove to experience design from all different angles of the industry. This led her to study traditional artisans' techniques with a ready-to-wear studio in Mexico City, design jewelry for a women's cooperative in the "thousand hills" of Rwanda, as well as apprentice in the atelier of Viktor & Rolf in Amsterdam and Boudicca in London.
